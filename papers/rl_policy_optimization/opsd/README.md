## Files

- `s01_introduction.md` — Motivates **On-Policy Self-Distillation (OPSD)**: a single LLM acts as both teacher and student, where the teacher policy $p_T(\cdot\mid x,y^\star)$ conditions on privileged ground-truth solutions while the student $p_S(\cdot\mid x)$ sees only the question. Training minimizes the per-token divergence between them over the student's own rollouts (Eq. 1), combining on-policy data, dense per-token feedback, use of ground-truth $y^\star$, and **no external teacher** — properties no prior method (SFT, RLVR/GRPO, standard on-policy distillation) achieves simultaneously. Inspired by the intuition that rationalizing a known answer is easier than generating it.
- `s02_background.md` — Formal background on **knowledge distillation** for autoregressive LLMs (supervised/off-policy Eq. 2 vs. on-policy Eq. 3, the latter connecting to imitation learning) and **RLVR**. Recaps GRPO's group-normalized advantage $A_i=(r_i-\text{mean})/\text{std}$ (Eq. 4) and clipped objective (Eq. 5) through a value-function lens, and names RLVR's two limitations: sparse sequence-level reward, and zero-advantage gradient collapse when all group samples share the same reward.
- `s03_methods.md` — The core method. Defines teacher/student as the same parameters $\theta$ under different conditioning context; the student samples on-policy, then both evaluate the trajectory in one forward pass each. Presents the **full-vocabulary logit distillation** objective (Eqs. 6–8, gradients only through the student, teacher fixed to the initial policy), the generalized $\operatorname{JSD}_\beta$, **per-token pointwise divergence clipping** (clip vocabulary-level $f$-divergence contributions at $\tau$ to stop stylistic tokens dominating math tokens), and an **alternative sampled-token policy-gradient objective** (Eq. 9) with stop-gradient advantage. Frames OPSD as a dense-reward policy gradient vs. STaR's sequence-level signal.
- `s04_experiments.md` — Evaluates `Qwen3-1.7B/4B/8B` on `AIME 2024/2025` and `HMMT 2025`, trained on up to 30K `OpenThoughts` math pairs. OPSD **matches or exceeds GRPO at every scale** while using a single rollout, ~100 steps, and only **1024 tokens per problem** vs. GRPO's 8×16k; SFT degrades due to short reasoning traces. Ablations: **forward KL** beats reverse-KL/JSD (36.7→43.9 on AIME25 at step 50); **TM-off student / TM-on teacher** maximizes math-token KL; **pointwise clipping** prevents collapse; longer generation (1024→4096) doesn't help (early tokens matter most); **full-vocabulary** distillation beats sampled-token but costs more memory.
- `s05_related_work_conclusion.md` — Positions OPSD against LLM self-training (context distillation, ReST, STaR, in-context editing) and on-policy distillation (GKD, speculative KD, MiniLLM), emphasizing it is **on-policy soft distillation on the student's own rollouts** needing no external teacher. Notes concurrent SDPO and SDFT. Concludes OPSD beats SFT and matches/exceeds GRPO with far better sample efficiency. Includes the Impact Statement.
- `s06_references.md` — Full reference list (49 author–year entries indexed alphabetically, plus 3 recovered entries [50]–[52] that running-header artifacts had merged into neighbors: SDPO/Hübotter, s1/Muennighoff, MetaMath/Yu).
- `s07_appendix.md` — Appendices A–D: (A) limitations (≤8B scale) and future directions (correctness verification, difficulty curricula); (B) experimental details (8×A100/H100, LoRA, AdamW, bf16, Flash-Attention-2, untuned $\tau$); (C) the explicit **style-token and math-token keyword lists** used for KL category analysis; (D) the formal **policy-gradient interpretation** showing STaR is a sequence-level binary-reward policy gradient (Eqs. 10–11) while OPSD is a dense per-token-reward policy gradient.
