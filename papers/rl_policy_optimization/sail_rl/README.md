# Guiding MLLMs in When and How to Think via Dual-Reward RL Tuning
OpenReview: 14654 | Anonymous Author(s) (NeurIPS 2026 submission)
Code: —

## Files

- `s01_introduction.md` — Covers the abstract and full Introduction. Introduces SAIL-RL, a dual-reward RL post-training framework for MLLMs that addresses two challenges: outcome-only supervision producing answers without sound reasoning, and uniform thinking strategies causing overthinking on simple tasks and underthinking on complex ones. The Thinking Reward evaluates reasoning quality (logical coherence, factual grounding, answer consistency) while the Judging Reward adaptively determines when deep reasoning is needed.
- `s02_related_work.md` — Surveys Cognitive Paradigms in MLLMs (System 1 fast-perception vs. System 2 deliberative reasoning models inspired by DeepSeek-R1) and Multimodal Reinforcement Learning. Discusses the latency tradeoff of long-CoT reasoning and prior work that tackles efficiency and effectiveness separately, motivating the joint dual-reward approach.
- `s03_sail_rl_method.md` — The core method section. Details the Thinking Reward with three binary sub-dimensions ($d_1$ Logical Coherence, $d_2$ Factual Grounding, $d_3$ Answer Consistency) averaged into $\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{think}}$, the binary Judging Reward $d_{\mathrm{judge}}$ for adaptive reasoning, the multiplicative Cascading Reward System $\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{total}} = \alpha \cdot (\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{judge}} \cdot \mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{think}} \cdot \mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{answer}}) + (1-\alpha) \cdot \mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{format}}$, and the two-stage post-training strategy (LongCoT SFT + DAPO-based RL).
- `s04_experiments.md` — Comprehensive experiments across multimodal reasoning benchmarks (DynaMath, LogicVista, MathVerse, MathVision, MathVista, WeMath) and general multimodal benchmarks (MMMU, MMBench, MME, ChartQA, AI2D, Hallusion). Shows SAIL-VL2-Thinking achieves SOTA among open-source models at 8B scale and competitive performance against `GPT-4o`. Includes ablations on single-dimension reasoning, dual-RL reward system, reward mechanism analysis (cascading product vs. additive), and robustness across reward models and model scales.
- `s05_conclusion.md` — Summarizes SAIL-RL as a principled dual-reward RL framework for teaching MLLMs when and how to think. Notes two limitations: noisy rewards in out-of-domain scenarios and increased memory overhead/training variance from explicit reasoning traces.
- `s06_references.md` — Complete 44-entry reference list formatted as a table, spanning venues from NeurIPS/CVPR/ECCV/ACL to arXiv, years 2017–2025.
- `s07_appendix_implementation.md` — Implementation details covering the 400K-sample LongCoT data curation pipeline (cleaning, conditional annotation, quality filtering), the 70K-sample RL dataset (50K STEM + 20K General QA), and full training hyperparameters for both SFT (1 epoch, lr $1\times10^{-6}$, batch 1024) and RL (3 epochs, DAPO, lr $1\times10^{-6}$, batch 256, 5 rollouts, $\varepsilon \in [0.20, 0.28]$).
- `s08_appendix_reward_prompts.md` — Appendix B case studies on Thinking Reward logical fidelity and Judging Reward meta-cognitive awareness, plus Appendix C with four complete reward prompt templates (Judge Reward, Thinking Logic Reward, Thinking Consistency Reward, Thinking Hallucination Reward) used for the Gemini reward-judger, preserving all role definitions, input/output specifications, and analysis logic.
