# DeepSeek-V4: Towards Highly Efficient Million-Token Context Intelligence

Hugging Face: [deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-V4-Pro](https://huggingface.co/collections/deepseek-ai/deepseek-v4) | DeepSeek-AI (2026)
Code & Checkpoints: https://huggingface.co/collections/deepseek-ai/deepseek-v4

## Files

- `s00_s01_frontmatter_intro.md` — Title, abstract, and Section 1 Introduction of the DeepSeek-V4 tech report. Presents the two-model MoE series (`DeepSeek-V4-Pro`: 1.6T/49B activated, `DeepSeek-V4-Flash`: 284B/13B activated) supporting 1M-token contexts. Headline efficiency: only **27% FLOPs** and **10% KV cache** vs. `DeepSeek-V3.2`. Introduces the three architectural innovations — hybrid attention (CSA + HCA), **Manifold-Constrained Hyper-Connections (mHC)**, and **Muon optimizer** — alongside infrastructure optimizations and the two-stage post-training paradigm (specialist training via SFT+GRPO, then on-policy distillation). Includes the "Summary of Core Evaluation Results" with benchmarks across Knowledge, Reasoning, Agent, Long-Context, and Pro vs. Flash comparisons.

- `s02_architecture.md` — Full transcription of Section 2 Architecture. Covers inherited designs from DeepSeek-V3 (DeepSeekMoE with `Sqrt(Softplus(·))` activation and Hash routing, MTP), the novel **Manifold-Constrained Hyper-Connections (mHC)** that constrain residual matrices to the **Birkhoff polytope** via Sinkhorn-Knopp for stable deep-layer training, the **hybrid attention system** combining **Compressed Sparse Attention (CSA)** with lightning indexer sparse selection and **Heavily Compressed Attention (HCA)** with aggressive KV compression, shared KV MQA, grouped output projection, partial RoPE, sliding window attention, and attention sinks. Also details the **Muon optimizer** with hybrid Newton-Schulz iterations.

- `s03_infrastructure.md` — Section 3 covering all infrastructure optimizations for training and inference. Includes `MegaMoE^2` fine-grained expert parallelism with **1.50–1.96×** speedup via wave-based communication-computation overlap, **TileLang** DSL for efficient kernel development with Host Codegen and Z3 SMT analysis, batch-invariant/deterministic kernel libraries for bitwise reproducibility, training framework enhancements (Muon + ZeRO, mHC with only **6.7%** overhead, contextual parallelism for compressed attention, **TorchFX** tensor-level activation checkpointing), and the inference framework's heterogeneous KV cache layout with three on-disk SWA KV cache storage strategies.

- `s04_pretraining.md` — Section 4 detailing the pre-training pipeline: a **32T+ token** corpus with improved filtering, agentic coding data, expanded multilingual coverage, long-document curation, and sample-level attention masking. Full hyperparameter tables for both Flash and Pro variants. Introduces two training stability techniques — **Anticipatory Routing** (decoupling backbone/routing updates) and **SwiGLU Clamping** (clamping to `[-10, 10]`). Evaluation results across four benchmark dimensions show `DeepSeek-V4-Pro-Base` achieving near-universal dominance and `DeepSeek-V4-Flash-Base` surpassing the larger `V3.2-Base`.

- `s05_posttraining.md` — Complete Section 5 on post-training methodology. Describes the two-stage pipeline where **On-Policy Distillation (OPD)** replaces mixed RL to merge 10+ domain specialists into a unified model, specialist training with **GRPO**, three reasoning effort modes (Non-think/Think High/Think Max), and the novel **Generative Reward Model**. Documents **FP4 QAT** with lossless FP4-to-FP8 dequantization, efficient teacher scheduling for full-vocabulary OPD, preemptible/fault-tolerant rollout with token-granular WAL, million-token RL scaling, and the **DSec** sandbox platform. Detailed benchmark tables show `DeepSeek-V4-Pro-Max` establishing new open-source SOTA. Real-world task evaluations cover Chinese writing, search (RAG vs. agentic), white-collar professional tasks, and internal R&D coding.

- `s06_conclusion.md` — Section 6: Conclusion, Limitations, and Future Directions. Summarizes achievements (million-token native context, open-model SOTA, cost-efficient Flash-Max), honestly acknowledges architectural complexity as a risk-mitigation tradeoff, and outlines forward-looking goals: architecture simplification, training stability research, sparse embeddings, low-latency deployment, multimodal capabilities, and better data curation.

- `s07_references.md` — Full reference list of **103 cited works** formatted as a Markdown table, spanning venues from NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, ACL, EMNLP to arXiv, USENIX, and GitHub.

- `s08_appendix.md` — Appendix A (alphabetical author list across Research & Engineering and Business & Compliance teams, plus acknowledgments) and Appendix B (six evaluation tables covering Agentic Search vs. RAG comparisons, V4-Pro vs. V3.2 search Q&A, Chinese writing head-to-heads against `Gemini-3.1-Pro`, and complex instruction following vs. `Claude-Opus-4.5`).
